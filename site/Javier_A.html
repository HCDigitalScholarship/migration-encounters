Javier:        My step grandpa at the time, he had a construction company. He was like the foreman and he would bring me on the vacation times. He would bring me with him. I obviously didn't work like stressful labor, but he was a little harsh on me. Just trying to make me understand what life and work was all about.

Anita:        Any examples of what he did? What kind of things?

Javier:        Well, he always brought me around with him. I was always, always just strictly with him. He would never leave me with anybody else unless it was my stepdad. Yeah, it was hard at the time though.

Anita:        For a little guy [laughs].

Javier:        Yeah, it was. But it actually taught me a lot though for life in general.

Javier:        It taught me how hard it is to work, that particular part of life in general and that I didn't want to do it. Javier:        Yeah I don't mind doing physical labor, but I felt like that was just like, almost like punishment for heavy weights, long hours and like all of that. And then they separated and now it's just... To me it just seemed like a big waste of time in general for both of them.

Anita:        But do you think it was for economic reasons they hope to make it or?

Javier:        Yes, they thought it was going to be for economic reasons, but I don't think that their thinking was just specific to the idea though. At that time, but by the time I got there after I was 15 years old.

And then we're just drinking that. Then at the last day when we got there-
