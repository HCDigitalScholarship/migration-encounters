And we spent a good time remembering what it was, how it was. And they talk about, they tried to go back to the US, but now they tried to go back with papers, like me. I mean, if I tried to go back, I don't try to cross the border illegally again. Because, you know, right now, it's really dangerous and I don't want to take that risk no more. Not for me, for my daughters, and my parents. So that's different. You learn to think different at the second time.

And my uncle, I have a uncle who told me about the doctor. He's helping the people to lose weight. And I tried in Toluca right here in the state of Mexico. And he helped me a lot. A lot. Right now I'm 130 kilos and I'm still losing weight. And I'm trying to be, to be good. My health is first now.

Hugo:        Yeah, but at the first time, when I came in here, Mexico, I never hear about these kind of jobs, like a call center where I'm working now. And I work for my own. My actually girlfriend right now. But, I mean, if she don't want to call me and she don't receive my calls, that's because she don't love me, right? She don't love me no more.

Hugo:        And there's where there was, after six years here in Mexico, I feel alone. I start to be in depression. And I know this girl now who I was... I have a relationship now and I feel better. I take a second chance for me to feel better. And that's good. I'm even better now. That really, really better. After, I learn about this jobs, I start to working here in Mexico. On my first call center was, uhm… a teleperformance and I love it. The first time I love it. After that, I changed for that job when my mom has a eye surgery, she has like a three months.
