Lizzy :        This is Lizzy for our interview. So, for the interview let's start back at the beginning. You said you came to the US when you were in first grade or so?

Rodolfo:        Yeah, like seven, six years. Like I said, my grandpa was going through hard times when my mom left because they weren't that cool. So they left first. So, I stayed with him. I was still going to go to school but one day I heard him on the phone talking how was I going to do school, to do something. He had either to go to a job or do his stuff. He can't just leave me with my grandpa's mom. He was older. So, he did like, "Yeah, we'll go get him." And he told me, "You know what? They're going to come get you. You're going to live with your mom. You're going to go to the States and everything. Going to go to Disneyland and everything." I'm like, "Oh wow. She's cool." I was like, "Yeah."

You should go with him. I'm going to take you with your mom and everything." I'm like, "Well, that's okay." So, when we were leaving over there he told me, "Look, this is going to be some papers that you have to... tienes que estudiarlos hijo.You have to study them. It's just the name. The name, the age, where you were born and what you do over there. What you live for over there." I'm like, "Oh, okay."

Rodolfo:        And told me, "Just tell them you go to school. You live with your mom. Your mom works." And what else? "That name." I don't remember that name. I think it was like-

Lizzy:        Do you still remember now what the name was?

Rodolfo:        I think it was ______.

Lizzy:        Okay.

Rodolfo:        But I don't remember the last name. But it was ______. And he's like, "You're gonna go over there, you're gonna say that and you're gonna have the same age. Seven years, six years." And I'm like, "Okay."

Rodolfo:        Yes, but I don't remember it.
