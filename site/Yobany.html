Claudia:        Yeah. And how was crossing the border?

I didn't really know much, I just remember we were going with my aunts and we turned the States, because I remember a month before they had sent us a tape about there, they were, I think Yosemite Park, which is in California. It's a national forest, and they sent us a video of when they were under the waterfall, so we're like, "Wow, that's amazing. That's beautiful", you know? Me and my sisters were all amazed. "We don't see that here, we see", we call it the riverbeds, you know? The dirty water. I'd be like, "Oh, my God." That's what we see out here.

Yobany:        Yeah, we saw that and we decided... well, actually my mom was not supposed to, we were not supposed to leave the country, we were supposed to stay here. My dad was just going to go and work and send us money. But when we saw that tape, we got all excited. My mom decided to sell everything we had over here to kind of get for the coyote, you know the people in poyero and to get money for that, and also my dad sent us some other money so we could go ahead and cross over illegally. So I remember that day that we were leaving, it was like 5 o'clock in the afternoon.

Yobany:        I remember the house was empty already. I remember my mom said, "Come on, get in the car. They're going to take us to the airport because we're going to go to Tijuana." And I remember just, I had forgotten something. I told my mom, "Wait, I forgot something", so I went back in the house and I just remember looking around and I just saw the house empty. That kind of got me. I remember that instant imprinted on my mind. Then from there we left to Tijuana. We arrived at Tijuana at night. I remember we stayed in a hotel. This guy meet us up at a hotel. Actually, this guy, we meet up in a, it was in a market. I think it was... I forgot. It looked like a grocery store, like a big grocery store, I remember the guy met us there and my uncle met us there, too, but he has paper he carrying. He used to live in the States.

Yobany:        So, we met up with the coyote and then he told us, "You're going to be staying at this hotel. I'm going to come pick you guys up at this time at night so we can go ahead and cross the border." So, I remember I was just staying at the hotel. I remember I took a nap, woke up. The guy knocked on the door. He gave us, I guess, some, what do you call it? He gave us some identification, so he gave us an ID. He gave us three of them. I remember, oh, my little brother was barely born, he was only like months old. I remember we dressed him up as a girl. Yeah, we dressed up my little brother like a girl because we had a cousin in the States that was born over there that was the same age.

Yobany:        But they crossed my little brother with my uncle, my uncle crossed him, and so it was only us. It was me and my mom and my two sisters, and so we went ahead and I remember that night, the coyote came, he gave us our IDs. And I was just like, I remember when we got up to the line, he exactly told us what to say. He was like, "Were you in Mexico", I mean, "Why you came to Mexico for?" And we were like, "For a piñata, for a party." I remember he just looked at us, he started saying, my mom said, "For a piñata", and the immigration officer went ahead and gave a look to us. He started seeing our names, or the names that were on the IDs. I remember raising my hand, and then that was it. He went, "Go ahead."

Yobany:        And I remember just looking over to my mom and telling her, "Mamá, estamos en los Estados Unidos?” And my mom goes, “Cállate, cállate!" From that I just remember crossing. We got into a trolley, it was like a two four trolley that goes from, I think, San Diego all the way to California to La Placito Olvera. So I remember we got there. They came and picked us up, and we got to our new place and we were amazed. We were like, "Wow, this is so much different. Yes it is."
