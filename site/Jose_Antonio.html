Mexico City, Mexico

Jose Antonio:        I had a rough year.

Lizzy:        So this is Lizzy, I'm here with Jose Antonio for his interview. And so you said you don't want to talk about Mexico, so...

Jose Antonio:        I'll talk about it. I'll put it out there.

Jose Antonio:        I could start from the beginning, like how I got there.

Lizzy:        Okay.

Jose Antonio:        I was only seven years old. 1998. My parents went first, in 1997. It was my mom and dad, they went there first. Wilmington, Delaware. That's where they got to live first. And then they sent one of my uncles to come get me and my little brother around 1999. Yeah. And I was seven years old when they took us over there across the border. And we was living in San Francisco, California for like two months. And then from there they took us to Wilmington, Delaware.

Lizzy:        To Delaware. Wow.

Jose Antonio:        Yeah, Delaware, the first state.

Lizzy:        How was that compared to California?

Jose Antonio:        To California? It was pretty different. We had to go there because our parents was there, but I really liked California when I first got there.

Lizzy:        You didn't like California?

Jose Antonio:        I liked it.

Lizzy:        Oh, you did like it.

Jose Antonio:        Yeah. But my parents wanted us to go to Delaware, so they took us to Delaware. And that's where I grew up most of my life, in Delaware. That's where I got all my friends, I know everybody. I went to school. Love Delaware.

Lizzy:        Why did they want to go to Delaware? Did you have family?

Jose Antonio:        That's where they lived, my parents lived.

Lizzy:        Okay. Okay.

Jose Antonio:        Yeah, that's where they lived, my mom and dad. And that's where they was currently living at, and in 2003 they got separated. They kind of like  got split up. Relationship wasn't working anymore, so they separated. So my dad... I had a little sister that my dad had over there, she was three years old. She was born in Delaware. So when they got split up, my dad, he took my little sister and my brother, my younger brother that went with me to the States. He took them down here. He brung them back over here in 2003. So he left me with my mom by myself out there.

Lizzy:        Just you and your mom.

Jose Antonio:        Yeah. Then my mom got with somebody else, with another guy, and she started going out with him. She had a baby. She was pregnant. She had my little brother Angel, and then she was pregnant with my little sister Theresa. So she wasn't really paying no attention to my anymore, she was doing her, her life with him. And he didn't really like me like that, so I was out there. I'm going to say I grew up in the bad neighborhood at the time. There was a lot of... We got the Blacks and we got the Dominicans, Puerto Ricans, and then they got the Mexicans, whatever. But I stayed with my kind because they was... It was a must back then, there was a lot of gangs and stuff around the neighborhood. And going to school, getting bullied, and stuff like that. It was rough at the beginning, and then...

Lizzy:        When you say you stayed with your kind, do you mean with Mexicans?

Jose Antonio:        Like Mexicans, like Hispanics. Sometimes there was a couple Puerto Ricans that were cool with us.

Lizzy:        Did you hang out with Dominicans?

Jose Antonio:        Not really. Only Puerto Ricans. They didn't really like us, I don't know why. And there was a lot of gang stuff, I had that problem growing up. So I had to stick around a certain crew to be alright, you know? I only did that when I was young, but then I grew up, like age 18, the teachers started finding out that I was having problems at home, that my dad wasn't with my mom no more and that I was being in the streets too much. So they started finding out when I started going into juvenile hall. So they started to know that I was having issues, and they called the state on my mom and they knew that I was staying somewhere else, that I wasn't staying with my mom. I was underage. So they took over me and they put me in the foster home up in Dover. Dover, Delaware. They put me in a foster home there with a African-American dude. He was pretty cool.

Lizzy:        What led you to go to juvie?

Jose Antonio:        Being with the friends, stealing and stuff like that. Doing stupid stuff when you're a kid, 14, 15 years old. Stupid stuff. So it was just like...

Lizzy:        And was this ever part of a gang, or just...

Jose Antonio:        It was a like a part of the group, something like that. But as soon that I got turned 18 years old I kind of changed my life around. I tried to change. All my tattoos, I did them when I was a young kid. I was a teenager. So after that I didn't get no more tattoos. And after the age of 18 I finished, when I was in juvie, I did like a year or something. I was in ninth grade when they got me. I did tenth and eleventh in there. So when I got out they was going to put me back in school, my probation officer, because I was still in state custody. So I was going to go back to school, but then I started working. They gave me night schools. I couldn't go to normal school no more. They gave me night school and stuff to go and get my diploma and stuff like that, or-

Lizzy:        Because you were working during the day?

Jose Antonio:        Yeah. Or my GED. But then I just got a normal job. A job that I had to work every day. So I just stopped going to school.

Lizzy:        So you didn't have time to finish it?

Jose Antonio:        Yeah. I thought it wasn't going to help me at the time or nothing, like the school wasn’t going to help me, so I'm just going to start working. So I started working, and I was working for a while. Started landscaping, I started doing construction work, like foundations, how you start a house and they do the foundation, like the under. Like the basement, stuff like that. So I was doing that for a minute. And then I got with this girl, she's citizen. I got with this girl and we got together, and then we got in a relationship. And then we got married our fourth year together, we got married. We're still married right now. She could help me out, but she's been a really... She don't want to help me out at all. She's like a...

Jose Antonio:        Yeah, we still married.

Lizzy:        Are you still... Do you still consider yourself married? Or are you separated?

Jose Antonio:        We separated, but we stayed married. We never got divorced. I got with somebody else and I was still married. I told my partner, I was like, "Look, I'm married with somebody, she don't want to give me the divorce. I already talked to her, she blocked me on Facebook and everything, I don't know what to do and I don't want to..."

Jose Antonio:        No, we don't talk to each other at all. Because I can marry my fiancee that I'm with right now, because I got twins with her. I got twin baby girls with her.

Lizzy:        Here in Mexico?

Jose Antonio:        No in...

Lizzy:        In the US.

Jose Antonio:        In the US, in Miami.

Lizzy:        Okay.

Jose Antonio:        It's pretty cheap in Ohio. The rent.

That's been my goal since back then, and I still want to get back to the States and do the same thing like that. And I'm learning how to make a lot of Mexican food out here, so I'm...

Lizzy:        Learn while you're here and then take it back.

Jose Antonio:        So I'm going to take that out... Yeah, because some of the food that they got out here is not out there, and I seen that when I was out there in the restaurant. So It's not the same food like they got out here. And I came out here and I seen more food that I'd never seen, and I tried it, and I'm like, "It's pretty good." I'm like, "They don't got this out there. If I take this out there it's going to blow up. You know?

Lizzy:        So what kind of food would you want to have at your restaurant?

Jose Antonio:        Mexican food. Also authentic food. Kind of Americanize it, too, but have the real Mexican food, too. Have both so that I have all types of customers. I've got that in my head since a long time ago, but now...

Jose Antonio:        I'm still thinking about that, because Miami... If I fix my legal status I would like for it to be in Miami. Miami, Florida. Because out there there's no businesses like that, many places that got restaurants and stuff like that. So that's where I want it to be, but since... I'm going to see what happens later on in the future, but I really do want to do that still. I'm still young. I'm 27 years old, and hopefully I'll get through that all my 30s and come back and have something, have a house.

Lizzy:        So let's go back a little bit.

Jose Antonio:        All right.

Lizzy:        Could you tell me a little bit about the story of how you ended up leaving?

Jose Antonio:        All right. Then from... I'm going to tell you the whole thing, so...

Lizzy:        Okay.

Jose Antonio:        Then from Ohio I went to Illinois with her. She convinced me, we went out there to Illinois. I met her dad, her family, and I worked in the restaurant out there, too, for a little bit. And then we got separated. That's when we got separated, and we broke up, and it was like, you know what? We'll take a break and we'll see what's up later on."

Jose Antonio:        All right, so I went back to Delaware, and since all my friends are there and I grew up there, I went to school there. So I seen everybody and I started working. I was doing good. I had my car, I had my apartment, I was doing great by myself. And I really didn't need nobody because I always had my own back working. My mom, she lived in Miami, Florida at the time. She moved to Miami, Florida in 2008. So she took my little brothers with her. She always had her own independent life with the man that she got with. And me, I had made my own life up there. I grew up a little more up. I was 18 years old. I just worked by myself and tried to be independent. And I used to go visit my mom every once in a while, two months, and then go back up there to Delaware and stuff like that.

Jose Antonio:        Then I decided to go out there around Hurricane Irma. I don't know if you had heard about that.

Lizzy:        Yeah. Yeah.

Jose Antonio:        So when it went through Florida, I told my mom, "Look, you can come down here during the hurricane, because I don't want nothing to happen to you. I heard it's going to be really bad. I don't want nothing to happen to you. Can you please come down here?" I begged her to come down here. So then she went to Delaware and she stayed out there for a week. She grabbed a rental car because she fixed her residence already, so she's a resident now. But she can't help me out until she's a citizen. So...

Lizzy:        Right.

Jose Antonio:        All right, so she-

Lizzy:        How long has she been a resident?

Jose Antonio:        Recently. She just recently...

Lizzy:        Just got it.

Jose Antonio:        Yeah, like two years, probably. Yeah, because I wasn't even in Miami for two years until they busted me. When she went down there for Hurricane Irma, I didn't want her to go back driving by herself. She was like, "Can you go with me so you can help me drive?" I was just going to help her drive because I had a girlfriend in Delaware. I had my car, had my apartment. And one of my friends used to stay with me, so we used to split up the rent together, so it was good. And my mom told me, "You can come, if you want to help me drive down there you can stay in the house for a month and then you can come back," and I was like, "Okay."

Jose Antonio:        So I helped her drive all the way down there, and then I stayed in Miami, and then I seen some of my old friends that I seen when I used to go visit her. So I kind of stayed. They got me a job and I was like, I stayed with my mom. She needed help because at the time she had broken up with her partner, too, and she left him, too. And she had a problem, she used to go to work and she used to sell food. So she left the kids alone one time at the house, and somebody told on her and they took the kids from her. The state. And they didn't want to give them back. I used to go to court with her and see if she was going to get them back. They took full custody of them, the state, Florida state took custody of them. So she wasn't able to get them back, ever.

Lizzy:        She never got them back?

Jose Antonio:        No.

Jose Antonio:        Yes. She stayed by herself, so I decided to stay in Miami with her and help her out. We had an apartment together and I just helped my mom, because that's my mom. So I told my boy, "You know what? I worked in drywall. I used to install drywall, that was what I liked to do the most because... So I had a car accident out there. We had a car accident in 2011, I think it was. Had a car accident in December. It was Christmas time. We got out the road, it was icy. We was going to New Jersey, to a party out there, and the car got out of control, it slid, we hit a pole, and then we started flipping.

Jose Antonio:        Everybody was okay. It was five of us. One of my friends, he flew out the car and he landed under the car when it stopped. And then we thought he was dead. Jose Antonio:        And he was like, "I can't believe you all survived that because it was a pretty ugly crash." He was like, "I'm glad you're all all right." And he was the one that called all the ambulances and paramedics and stuff like that.

Jose Antonio:        Well, we're together, we had our own apartment, and then...

I was like, "Oh."
